## Become a micro blogger

Why everyone should micro-blog or at least leave a place on the internet that belongs to just you: the idea that having digital independence and having a place completely yours to share ideas, thoughts, and things you are working on or interested in without any filtering or flagging is amazing. Back in the day, people used to share thoughts on these tiny websites made simply in Markdown or HTML. Those sites often looked rough, but they were a direct piece of someone's brain - like a mini book that someone decided to put online. It's a cool way to learn about someone in a fun, honest way. You can build something yourself and publish it on GitHub, or, if you prefer hosted options, you can use services like [Bear Blog](http://bearblog.dev/) or [Micro.blog](https://micro.blog/) or something similar.

## Just build software for yourself

Nobody talks about this enough - you can just build software for yourself. Not to ship, not to impress anyone, just because you have a weird specific need and you want something that handles exactly that. A tool that tracks the one thing you actually care about. A script that automates the annoying task only you seem to have. Something that works exactly the way your brain works. AI makes this stupidly easy now - you don't even need to know how to code. You just describe what you want, vibe your way through it, and end up with a thing that's completely yours. Software you actually built for your actual life. That's kind of wild when you think about it.

## Your gadget doesn't need your email

Karpathy wrote about [why he loves the calculator](https://karpathy.bearblog.dev/i-love-calculator/) - it turns on, it does math, done. No login, no app, no bullshit. You bought it, it's yours. That used to just be how things worked. Now your air monitor won't tell you the air quality until you make an account. Your TV is collecting data while you watch it. We slowly went from owning things to renting access to things we already paid for. The calculator just does the thing. More stuff should just do the thing.
